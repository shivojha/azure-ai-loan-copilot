# Credit Score Guidelines for Home Loans

## Credit Score Ranges and Loan Options
Credit scores for mortgage lending use the FICO model and range from 300 to 850.
- 740 and above: Excellent. Qualifies for the best rates on all loan types.
- 700 to 739: Good. Qualifies for most loan products with competitive rates.
- 680 to 699: Fair-good. Conventional and FHA loans available; rates slightly higher.
- 640 to 679: Fair. FHA preferred; conventional may require larger down payment.
- 580 to 639: Below average. FHA with 3.5% down is the primary option.
- 500 to 579: Poor. FHA with 10% down may be available; most conventional lenders decline.
- Below 500: Very poor. Generally not eligible for traditional mortgage financing.

## How Lenders Use Credit Scores
Lenders pull credit reports from all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) and
use the middle score of the three for qualification. For joint applications, the lender
uses the lower of the two middle scores. The credit score affects both loan eligibility
and the interest rate offered. A higher score means lower perceived risk and a lower rate.

## How to Improve Credit Score Before Applying
Steps that can improve a credit score in the months before a mortgage application:
- Pay all bills on time — payment history is the largest factor at 35% of the FICO score
- Reduce credit card balances to below 30% of the credit limit (utilization is 30% of score)
- Avoid opening new credit accounts in the 6 to 12 months before applying
- Do not close old accounts — length of credit history matters
- Dispute any errors on your credit report with the reporting bureaus

## Credit Events and Waiting Periods
Major negative credit events require waiting periods before mortgage eligibility:
- Bankruptcy Chapter 7: 2 years after discharge for FHA; 4 years for conventional
- Bankruptcy Chapter 13: 1 year into repayment plan for FHA; 2 years after discharge for conventional
- Foreclosure: 3 years after for FHA; 7 years for conventional (3 years with extenuating circumstances)
- Short sale: 3 years for FHA; 4 years for conventional

## Credit Score vs Interest Rate Impact
The difference in rate between a 620 score and a 760 score can be 1% to 1.5% or more.
On a $400,000 loan, a 1% rate difference equals roughly $240 more per month and over
$85,000 in additional interest over 30 years. Improving your score before applying
can be one of the most financially significant steps a borrower can take.

## Rapid Rescore
Some lenders offer rapid rescore services that can update credit report data within
three to five business days, compared to the standard 30-day update cycle. This can
be useful if a borrower has recently paid down debt or resolved an error and needs
an updated score for loan approval. Rapid rescore is initiated by the lender, not
the borrower directly.
