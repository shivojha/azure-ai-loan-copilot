# Closing Costs

## What Are Closing Costs
Closing costs are fees and expenses paid at the closing of a real estate transaction,
in addition to the down payment. They cover the cost of processing, securing, and
transferring the property. Closing costs are paid by both buyers and sellers, though
the buyer's share is typically larger. Buyers should budget 2% to 5% of the loan
amount for closing costs.

## Lender Fees
Lender fees include the origination fee, which covers the cost of processing the loan
and typically ranges from 0.5% to 1% of the loan amount. Other lender fees include
underwriting fees ($400 to $900), application fees, and rate lock fees. Some lenders
advertise no-closing-cost loans, where fees are rolled into a higher interest rate.

## Third-Party Fees
Third-party fees are charged by service providers outside the lender. These include:
- Appraisal fee: $300 to $600 for a standard single-family home
- Title search and title insurance: $700 to $1,500 depending on the loan amount
- Home inspection: $300 to $500 (typically paid before closing)
- Survey fee: $300 to $700 if a new survey is required
- Attorney fees: $500 to $1,500 where attorneys are required at closing

## Prepaid Items and Escrow
Prepaid items are costs paid in advance at closing and include:
- Homeowners insurance premium (first year often required upfront)
- Prepaid interest covering the days between closing and the first payment due date
- Property tax escrow reserve (typically two to three months of estimated taxes)
- Mortgage insurance premium if applicable
Escrow accounts hold funds for ongoing tax and insurance payments and require an
initial deposit at closing.

## Seller Concessions
Sellers can agree to pay a portion of the buyer's closing costs, known as seller
concessions. FHA loans allow seller concessions up to 6% of the purchase price.
Conventional loans limit seller concessions to 3% when the down payment is less
than 10%, and up to 6% with a 10% or greater down payment.

## How to Reduce Closing Costs
Strategies to reduce closing costs include negotiating seller concessions, shopping
multiple lenders and comparing Loan Estimate forms, asking the lender about no-cost
options, rolling costs into the loan balance where permitted, and using lender credits
in exchange for a slightly higher interest rate.

## Closing Disclosure
Lenders are required to provide a Closing Disclosure at least three business days
before closing. The Closing Disclosure itemizes all fees, the final loan terms, and
the exact cash needed to close. Buyers should compare it carefully to the Loan Estimate
received early in the process and question any new or increased fees.
