# Conventional Loan Requirements

## Credit Score Requirements
Most conventional loans require a minimum credit score of 620. To qualify for the best
interest rates, a score of 740 or higher is typically needed. Borrowers with scores
between 620 and 739 may qualify but will pay higher rates or require larger down payments.
Conventional loans are not government-backed, so lender requirements vary more than FHA.

## Down Payment Requirements
The minimum down payment for a conventional loan is typically 3% for first-time homebuyers
through programs such as Fannie Mae HomeReady or Freddie Mac Home Possible. Standard
conventional loans require 5% down. A 20% down payment eliminates the requirement for
private mortgage insurance (PMI). Down payment funds may come from savings, gifts, or
eligible assistance programs.

## Private Mortgage Insurance
PMI is required when the down payment is less than 20% of the purchase price. PMI
typically costs between 0.5% and 1.5% of the loan amount annually, divided into monthly
payments. Unlike FHA MIP, PMI can be cancelled once the borrower reaches 20% equity
in the home. Lenders are required to cancel PMI automatically at 22% equity.

## Debt-to-Income Ratio
Standard conventional loan guidelines allow a maximum DTI of 45%. With strong compensating
factors such as excellent credit or significant reserves, some loans can be approved up
to 50% DTI. Front-end DTI (housing costs only) is typically limited to 28%.

## Loan Limits
Conforming conventional loan limits for 2026 are $766,550 for a single-family home in most
areas. High-cost areas qualify for limits up to $1,149,825. Loans above the conforming
limit are called jumbo loans and carry stricter requirements including higher credit scores
and larger down payments.

## Income and Employment
Borrowers must demonstrate stable employment, typically two years of continuous employment
history. Self-employed borrowers generally need two years of tax returns. Acceptable income
sources include salary, wages, bonuses, rental income, and certain investment income.
All income must be documented and verified.
