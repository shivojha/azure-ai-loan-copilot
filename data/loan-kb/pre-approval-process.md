# Pre-Approval Process

## Pre-Qualification vs Pre-Approval
Pre-qualification is an informal estimate based on self-reported financial information.
It does not involve a credit check and carries little weight with sellers.
Pre-approval is a formal review where the lender verifies income, assets, and credit.
A pre-approval letter shows sellers you are a serious buyer and gives a reliable estimate
of how much you can borrow. Most sellers and agents require pre-approval before accepting offers.

## Documents Required for Pre-Approval
To get pre-approved, most lenders require the following documents:
- Recent pay stubs covering the last 30 days
- W-2 forms from the last two years
- Federal tax returns from the last two years
- Bank statements for the last two to three months (all accounts)
- Photo ID such as a driver's license or passport
- Social Security number for credit check authorization
- Employment history for the last two years
- Documentation of any additional income sources (rental income, bonuses, alimony)

## Self-Employed Borrowers
Self-employed borrowers typically need to provide two years of personal and business tax
returns, a year-to-date profit and loss statement, and business bank statements. Some
lenders offer bank statement loans that use 12 to 24 months of bank deposits instead
of tax returns to verify income.

## How Long Pre-Approval Takes
Initial pre-approval decisions often come within one to three business days once all
documents are submitted. Some lenders offer same-day decisions. The pre-approval letter
is typically valid for 60 to 90 days. If your letter expires before closing, you may
need to resubmit updated financial documents.

## Impact on Credit Score
A mortgage pre-approval requires a hard credit inquiry, which may lower your credit score
by a few points temporarily. Multiple mortgage inquiries within a 14 to 45 day window
are typically treated as a single inquiry under FICO scoring models, so shopping multiple
lenders within that window minimizes the credit impact.

## Conditional Approval
Conditional approval means the lender is prepared to approve the loan subject to specific
conditions such as a satisfactory appraisal, proof of homeowners insurance, or resolution
of a credit issue. Meeting all conditions converts the conditional approval to a full approval.
