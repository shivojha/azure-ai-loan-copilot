# FHA Loan Requirements

## Credit Score Requirements
FHA loans require a minimum credit score of 580 to qualify for the 3.5% down payment option.
Borrowers with credit scores between 500 and 579 may still qualify but must provide a minimum
10% down payment. Scores below 500 are not eligible for FHA financing.
Individual lenders may impose overlay requirements above the FHA minimums.

## Down Payment Requirements
The minimum down payment for an FHA loan is 3.5% of the purchase price for borrowers with
a credit score of 580 or higher. For scores between 500 and 579, the minimum is 10%.
Down payment funds may come from savings, gifts from family members, or approved down
payment assistance programs. Gift funds must be documented with a gift letter.

## Debt-to-Income Ratio
FHA guidelines generally allow a maximum front-end DTI of 31% and a back-end DTI of 43%.
Front-end DTI is the monthly housing payment divided by gross monthly income.
Back-end DTI includes all monthly debt obligations. Some lenders allow back-end DTI up to
50% with compensating factors such as significant cash reserves or a strong credit score.

## Loan Limits
FHA loan limits vary by county and property type. For 2026, the standard limit for a
single-family home is $498,257 in most areas. High-cost areas can qualify for limits up
to $1,149,825. Alaska, Hawaii, Guam, and the US Virgin Islands have higher ceilings.
Loan amounts above these limits require a jumbo or conventional loan.

## Mortgage Insurance Premium
FHA loans require two types of mortgage insurance. An upfront MIP of 1.75% of the loan
amount is due at closing and can be rolled into the loan. An annual MIP ranging from
0.45% to 1.05% is divided into monthly payments. MIP is required for the life of the
loan if the down payment is less than 10%. With a 10% or greater down payment, MIP
can be removed after 11 years.

## Property Requirements
The property must meet FHA minimum property standards. It must be the borrower's
primary residence. Condominiums must be on the FHA-approved condo list. The property
must undergo an FHA appraisal performed by an FHA-approved appraiser.
